Journey

Through the Ten Treasures of Turkey

Natural Wonder

Pamukkale

01

The "Cotton Castle" is a surreal landscape of white mineral terraces and warm, cascading thermal waters. Visitors can walk on the travertine formations, with the ruins of Greco-Roman Hierapolis watching from above.

Architectural Masterpiece

Hagia Sophia

02

An icon of Istanbul, its massive dome was a marvel of engineering for a millennium. Stepping inside reveals a breathtaking blend of Christian mosaics and Islamic calligraphy, a testament to the empires it has served.

Cliffside Monastery

Sumela Monastery

03

Hidden high on a sheer cliff face in the Pontic Mountains, this monastery seems to defy gravity. The journey through misty mountains to reach it is as breathtaking as the vibrant frescoes inside.

Ancient Roman City

Ephesus

04

Walk the marble streets of one of the world's most impressive archaeological sites. See the Library of Celsus and the Great Theatre, where every stone whispers stories of the Roman Empire’s grandeur.

Ottoman Landmark

Blue Mosque

05

Facing Hagia Sophia, its six elegant minarets and cascading domes dominate the skyline. Inside, over 20,000 blue İznik tiles create a dazzling display of light and artistry.

Imperial Residence

Topkapi Palace

06

For four centuries, this vast complex was the residence of Ottoman sultans. Explore its lush courtyards and ornate chambers to enter a real-life story from "One Thousand and One Nights."

Mythical Summit

Mount Nemrut

07

This mysterious mountain is crowned with colossal stone heads of ancient gods and kings. The climb is rewarded with one of the most dramatic and unforgettable sunrises in the world.

Monastic Complex

Göreme Museum

08

A UNESCO World Heritage site, this is a vast complex of monasteries carved into volcanic rock, featuring churches and chapels with well-preserved medieval frescoes depicting scenes from the Bible.

Theatre of nature

Kemer, Antalya

09

Nestled gracefully between the towering Taurus Mountains and the crystal-clear waters of the Mediterranean Sea, Kemer is a place where nature reveals its most breathtaking harmony. The landscape blends lush green pine forests, golden beaches, and rugged cliffs, creating a picture-perfect view at every turn.

Otherworldly Landscape

Cappadocia

10

In the heart of central Turkey lies a landscape that looks almost otherworldly. The “fairy chimneys” were created by volcanic eruptions, and a sunrise hot air balloon ride here is an unforgettable, magical experience.
